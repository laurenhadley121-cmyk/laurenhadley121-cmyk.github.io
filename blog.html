Reflections on Therapy

Approach to Therapy

I approach therapy with a focus on creating a safe and supportive environment for my clients. I work to build trust and rapport, ensuring that each individual feels heard and understood. I approach therapy through a systemic lens, considering the interconnectedness of individuals within their families and communities. My hope is to help clients discover who they truly are and leave sessions with a growing sense of joy, self-love, and confidence in who they are becoming.

Therapy Coursework

I am currently in my internship year at Thomas Jefferson University where I am completing my Master's in Couples and Family Therapy. Some examples of classes that I've taken include Theory and Practice of Family Therapy, Theory and Practice of Couples Therapy, Intro to Sex Therapy, Human Sexuality Studies, and Assessment and Mental Health Diagnosis and Treatment. I came to this work through my prior experience working with students and families in educational settings.
